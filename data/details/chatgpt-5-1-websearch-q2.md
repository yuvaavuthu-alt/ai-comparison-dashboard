2. Plan pricing for 4 lines (this matters a lot)

For a family of four, the plan tier often matters more than the small differences between phones.

Recent public estimates (with Auto Pay, 4 lines):  ￼
	•	Unlimited Welcome ≈ $30/line → ~$120/mo for 4 lines.
	•	Unlimited Plus ≈ $45/line → ~$180/mo for 4 lines.
	•	Unlimited Ultimate ≈ $55/line → ~$220/mo for 4 lines.

So if you move one line from Welcome → Plus, you’re roughly adding $15/mo for that line; Welcome → Ultimate is $25/mo more per line.

For 2 upgraded lines over 36 months, the extra plan cost is roughly:
	•	Welcome → Plus, 2 lines:
	•	2 × $15 × 36 ≈ $1,080.
	•	Welcome → Ultimate, 2 lines:
	•	2 × $25 × 36 ≈ $1,800.
	•	Plus → Ultimate, 2 lines:
	•	extra $10/line/mo → 2 × $10 × 36 = $720.

Reddit folks have actually done similar math on the 830 vs 1100 iPhone credits: the extra plan cost for Ultimate often outweighs the extra promo.  ￼

Scenario C – Move 2 lines to Unlimited Ultimate, 2× iPhone 17 Pro

So Unlimited Ultimate is clearly not the lowest-cost path just to get “free” iPhones. The plan upgrade cost eats the extra device savings.

For iPhones with two tier-1 trade-ins, the lowest raw 36-month cost is either:
	•	Stay on Unlimited Welcome and take the smaller trade-in credits (cheapest cash out), or
